iPod Nano
2008

iPod Nano(4th generation) Navigation : Click Wheel Capacity : 8 and 16 GB Model number and date of introduction: A1285, september 2008. The new iPod Nano differs from the previous models by: its larger screen, its curved surface, its oval shape when viewed from above or below, the accelerometer used by the “shake” function.

The shaped change to match the first and second generation iPod nano, with the vertical form being preferred by iPod nano users The screen of definition 320 × 240 pixels for 2 inches is curved. The main change with the arrival of iPod nano is the use of flash memory instead of the hard drive used in other models.

The advantages of this memory are the absence of mechanical parts, which makes the iPod nano more robust and usable in more situations (jogging…), and its size which makes it possible to design a much smaller iPod. In return, this medium is more expensive, the available memory is therefore less.

The battery life is 24 hours in audio mode and 4 hours in video mode. This version also allows greater accessibility for the visually impaired by integrating vocal menus. The aluminum and glass making up the structure of the 4th generation iPod nano are made with recyclable materials.
